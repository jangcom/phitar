Energy bin synchronization between tallies and cross sections¶

by J. Jang

  • Last: 2020-05-10
  • First: 2020-05-05

Introduction¶

PHITS tally energy bins are expressed as pairs of e-lower and e-upper, whereas cross sections are expressed as functions of energy points. This resulted in slight differences between the energy bins of the two datasets until phitar v1.03. The two energy bins have now been synchronized in phitar v1.04; the method and testing results are presented in this notebook.

Problem statement¶

As suggested by the above plot, the array indices of the two data do not necessarily match, resulting in slight differences in the pointwise multiplication. Realizing this discrepancy, the author deciced to modify the interpolation of fluence data, while keeping the interpolation of cross sections intact.

Solution¶

A simple solution is to make the midpoint of a tally energy bin equivalent to the energy point of a cross section. In this case, what we need to calculate are the e-lower of the first energy bin, or emin, and the e-upper of the last energy bin, or emax.

First, let's obtain emin. By the above statement, it should hold that

\[ \frac{e_{\mathrm{lower}} + e_{\mathrm{upper}}} {2} = e_{\mathrm{point}} \]

A tally energy bin shows that eupper is a function of elower, or vice versa:

e-lower e-upper Random data
0 8.0 8.5 0.109763
1 8.5 9.0 0.143038
2 9.0 9.5 0.120553
3 9.5 10.0 0.108977
4 10.0 10.5 0.084731
\[ e_{\mathrm{upper}} = e_{\mathrm{lower}} + \mathrm{d}e \]

We can therefore obtain

\[ \frac{2e_{\mathrm{lower}} + \mathrm{d}e} {2} = e_{\mathrm{point}} \]

Because we are trying to obtain emin, it holds that elower = emin and epoint = estart (estart is the start energy point of a cross section) and we obtain:

\[ \frac{2e_{\mathrm{min}} + \mathrm{d}e} {2} = e_{\mathrm{start}} \]

emin is therefore

\[ e_{\mathrm{min}} = e_{\mathrm{start}} - 0.5 \mathrm{d}e \] of which d$e$ is \[ \mathrm{d}e = \frac{e_{\mathrm{stop}} - e_{\mathrm{start}}} {n_{\mathrm{e}} - 1} \] where $e_{\mathrm{stop}}$ is the highest source energy, and $n_{\mathrm{e}}$ is the number of energy bins assigned by the user.

Now that we know de and emin, the corresponding emax can be calculated as

\[ e_{\mathrm{max}} = e_{\mathrm{min}} + n_{\mathrm{e}} \mathrm{d}e \]

Note that the ne should NOT be the typical linear spacing like:

\[ e_{\mathrm{max}} = e_{\mathrm{min}} + (n_{\mathrm{e}} - 1) \mathrm{d}e \] where the $-1$ is for $e_{\mathrm{min}}$. This is because PHITS will interpolate the fluence data for $n_{\mathrm{e}} + 1$:

Typical linear interpolation up to ne

  • (ne - 1) required for emin to become emax ... (ne - 1) + 1 = ne

PHITS linear interpolation up to ne + 1

  • (ne) required for emin to become emax ... (ne) + 1 = ne + 1

In phitar v1.04, the above solution has been implemented in a routine called determ_tally_emin_emax(). Now, let's try the solution.

Summary¶

  • Until phitar v1.03, there have been discrepancies between the energy bins of PHITS fluences and cross sections.
  • In phitar v1.04, the energy bin synchronization was carried out, as explained in this notebook.
